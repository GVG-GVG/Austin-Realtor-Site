As Iowa's state capital and largest city, Des Moines offers unparalleled affordability combined with urban amenities. Whether you're seeking downtown loft living, historic neighborhood charm, or affordable family neighborhoods, Des Moines delivers exceptional value with a vibrant cultural scene and strong economic foundation.

Distinctive Neighborhoods

East Village (Median: $252K)

Des Moines' trendiest neighborhood showcases new restaurants, independent shops, galleries, and loft conversions. Young professionals and creative entrepreneurs flock to East Village for its walkability, authentic urban energy, and strong sense of community. Gentrification is ongoing, making this an excellent investment opportunity. Properties appreciate as the neighborhood matures and develops.

Sherman Hill (Median: $330K)

A stunning historic district filled with meticulously preserved Victorian and Craftsman homes. Tree-lined streets, architectural details, and neighborhood character define Sherman Hill. Residents appreciate the area's established, mature feel and proximity to downtown. Recent restoration investments show strong market appreciation. Perfect for buyers seeking historic charm and neighborhood prestige.

Downtown (Median: $262K)

Modern urban living in the heart of Des Moines. Loft apartments in converted buildings, new construction condos, and mixed-use developments attract young professionals and empty-nesters. Walking to restaurants, theaters, and offices appeals to those seeking urban convenience. The downtown market is experiencing strong growth as more people embrace city living.

Beaverdale

A charming, established residential neighborhood with strong schools and stable family demographics. Tree-lined streets, quiet character, and accessible prices make Beaverdale attractive to families. The neighborhood maintains its charm while remaining close to downtown and commercial districts. Strong community pride and active neighborhood association enhance quality of life.

Education & Schools

Des Moines Public Schools

Coverage Area: All of Des Moines and surrounding areas

System Overview: Iowa's largest school district serving thousands of students. The system serves a diverse student population and emphasizes inclusive education, language programs, and student support services.

Notable Programs: Career technical education pathways, English language learner support, special education services, and diverse extracurricular offerings ensure students find opportunities matching their needs and interests.

Private & Charter Options

Diversity of Choice: Des Moines offers private schools, charter schools, and magnet programs providing families educational variety. Options include college preparatory institutions, arts-focused schools, and specialized academic programs.

Competitive Environment: The presence of multiple educational providers creates competitive quality improvements across all systems. Families benefit from the range of educational philosophies and approaches available.

Affordability Advantage: Unlike suburban areas, Des Moines' lower home prices reduce the financial barriers to accessing desirable school districts or private education, increasing overall educational accessibility.

What Makes Des Moines Special

Cultural Attractions

Home to the world-famous Sculpture Park, Pappajohn Sculpture Park, and performing arts centers. Des Moines' cultural scene rivals much larger cities. Museums, galleries, theaters, and concert venues provide endless entertainment and cultural enrichment.

Court Avenue District

The city's premier entertainment and dining destination features live music venues, craft breweries, upscale restaurants, and nightlife. Court Avenue vibrates with energy and attracts diverse crowds. Living near Court Avenue puts culture and entertainment at your doorstep.

Unbeatable Affordability

At $200K median price, Des Moines offers real estate value impossible to find in similarly vibrant cities nationwide. Your dollars stretch further, potentially allowing larger homes, better neighborhoods, or meaningful savings compared to coasts or other metros.

Historic Architecture

Des Moines' neighborhoods showcase beautiful architecture from multiple eras. Preservation efforts maintain these historical gems while allowing them to evolve. For architecture enthusiasts, Des Moines' diverse styles offer endless appreciation and study.

Economic Hub

As the state capital, Des Moines hosts significant government employment plus strong presence of financial services, insurance, publishing, and technology companies. Economic diversity creates employment stability and growth opportunities for professionals.

Active River Valley

The Des Moines and Raccoon River valleys provide trails, parks, and natural recreation. The river corridor is undergoing revitalization, creating new walkable spaces. Urban parks and trail access add to quality of life and property appeal.
